Why Logistics?

Home health care service delivery is a logistics problem. Did you know you were in a logistics business? Do you view it that way? We think you should. In 2009 the National Association for Homecare and Hospice (NAHC) wrote a paper comparing the mileage driven by UPS worldwide to clinicians in the home care industry. They found that UPS drove 2 Billion miles compared to over 5 Billion miles home care clinicians drove. That’s more than twice as much as the largest logistics company in the world! And the need for home care services is only growing.

Logistics in home health care is unique and challenging. Unlike for a UPS or a FEDEX where delivery can be largely at any time and by any personnel, home health care requires precision delivery – the right resource at the right time. This need for a precision has not been effectively met by tools that help agencies provide home care precisely. Rather, agencies are often left to meet their growing demand for patient care against tight resources through very rudimentary means that rely on the expertise and knowledge of scheduling personnel...making the process more art than science, more manual than automated. This leaves much room for improvement.

Our Solution

Our software enables the optimization of a home care agency's service delivery (supply) against its patient needs (demand). We approach the home care service deliver problem as a complex logistics problem, requiring the delivery of the right service, by the right care provider, at the right time. The staff recommendation engines makes it easy for schedulers to make fast and consistent decisions. We accomplish this by providing the right information to the scheduling staff along with recommendations to help align decisions with company goals.

The solution is designed to assist in the generation a schedule of care that balances a home care agency’s patient demand with its care provider supply in a manner that meets the agency’s business priorities (utilization of staff, salary vs FFS, least miles driven, etc). HCI recognizes that each home care agency situation is different. Agencies have fine grained control of the algorithms via easy to use sliders to produce the optimal schedule of care for each patient.

With our solution, agency staff can influence the generation of schedules and visit assignments through the prioritization of key business drivers such as: Patient proximity to clinician and clinician’s other patients, distribution of assignments by clinician or pay type, consistency of clinician-to-patient assignments, degree to which clinician hours are pre-assigned versus held in reserve and much more.

Automation

The HCI logistics solution can be configured to integrate with the agency EHR to prevent the unnecessary duplication of data entry. The integration can be configured as a one-way stream from the agency EHR to provide visit assignment recommendations or a two-way integration that wil automatically send all visit assignments and associated personnel back to the EHR in real-time.

Once you are confident in the manual visit assignment recommendations, you can turn on automatics assignments to realize the full benefits of the solution. All visits that meet your defined visit thresholds can be automatically assigned. Agencies can choose to manage their scheduling workload from a centralized location, reducing scheduler resources, while ensuring the agency initiatives are being enforced.

The HCI Auto-Contact solution automates routine calling tasks to streamline agency contact campaigns. Most agencies believe that contacting their patients before the scheduled visit helps to reduce missed visits. Unfortunately, the burden for this initiative falls on the clinicians, reducing their overall satisfaction. Our Auto-Contact solution calls the patient and allows them to press ‘1’ to confirm the visit or ‘2’ to decline. Other agency contact campaigns include: Post-visit patient satisfaction surveys and clinician visit acceptance / rejection confirmation.

SCHEDULER TOOLS

Schedulers use the recommendation engine to alleviate the guesswork associated with scheduling and assigning visits to clinicians. The organization’s initiatives and goals can be consistently measured while automation ensures effectiveness and efficiency.

RECOMMENDATION ENGINE

The clinician recommendation engine analyzes visit types, distances between patients, clinician availability, patient/staff preferences, and much more to generate optimal clinician assignments. Same day ad-hoc visit assignments evaluate the clinicians current driving route to identify the best clinician for the visit.

MANAGEMENT TOOLS

Managers can use a number of real-time reporting and analytical tools to oversee branch operations and take action. Management can use HCI to align their agencies responses to the ever-changing clinical demands of home care.

PRIMARY CLINICIAN / CASE MANAGER

Identify and assign a primary clinician (or case manager) for the patient. The goal is to assign the best clinician that can complete up to 100% of the visits for the patient’s episode to improve continuity of care. You can assign a primary and alternate clinician for each discipline.

OPTIONAL MOBILE APPLICATION

Clinicians have an intuitive mobile application to manage their daily schedule. Clinicians can view their driving route with turn-by-turn directions, capture and report mileage. Optionally, they can move, cancel and confirm visits, based on security roles.

ASSIGNMENT ALGORITHIMS

HCI utilizes a number of algorithims when evaluating clinicians for visit assignments. The application recommends the best clinician to complete a single visit, or visits for a patient's entire episode.
